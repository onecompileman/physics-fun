What is Acceleration?

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity of an object with respect to time. An object's acceleration is the net result of all forces acting on the object, as described by Newton's Second Law. The SI unit for acceleration is metre per second squared

Compute for Acceleration

Formula: Acceleration (m/s 2) = Change in Velocity (m/s) / Time (s)
Example: A rocket flying at 200 km/h and accelerates to 250 km/h in just 2 seconds , what is its acceleration?
  • Acceleration = Change in Velocity (m/s) / t (s)
  • = (250 km/h - 200 km/h) / 2 seconds
